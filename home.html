Introduction to Pokémon Emerald

Pokémon Emerald is a remastered version of Pokémon Ruby and Sapphire, released in 2005 for the Game Boy Advance. It combines elements from both games, featuring a unified storyline that resolves the conflicts between Team Aqua and Team Magma. The game follows a young Trainer who sets out to become the Pokémon Champion, uncovering ancient legends involving the mythical Pokémon Kyogre, Groudon, and Rayquaza. With enhanced graphics, new areas like the Battle Frontier, and a deeper narrative, Pokémon Emerald offers an expanded experience in the tropical Hoenn region.

Lists of Known Pokémons in Pokémon Emerald

Pokémon Emerald's Pokédex includes several Pokémon not available in the original Ruby and Sapphire versions. These additions enhance the game's roster and tie into its expanded story and Battle Frontier. Here are some key newly added Pokémon:

  • Jirachi: A mythical Steel/Psychic-type Pokémon known as the "Wish Pokémon," obtainable through special events.
  • Deoxys: A legendary Psychic-type Pokémon with multiple forms, central to the game's lore.
  • Chimecho: An evolved form of Chingling, a Psychic-type known for its bell-like appearance.
  • Tropius: A Grass/Flying-type Pokémon resembling a dinosaur with fruit on its neck.
  • Absol: A Dark-type Pokémon famous for its disaster-sensing abilities.
  • Wynaut: A pre-evolution of Wobbuffet, a Psychic-type with a playful nature.
  • Snorunt and Glalie: Ice-type Pokémon, with Glalie being Snorunt's evolution.
  • Spheal, Sealeo, and Walrein: A line of Ice/Water-type Pokémon resembling seals.
  • Clamperl, Huntail, and Gorebyss: Water-type evolutions of Clamperl, branching into different forms.
  • Relicanth: A Water/Rock-type fossil Pokémon.
  • Luvdisc: A Water-type Pokémon symbolizing love.
  • Bagon, Shelgon, and Salamence: A Dragon-type evolution line.
  • Beldum, Metang, and Metagross: Steel/Psychic-type Pokémon resembling robots.
  • Regirock, Regice, and Registeel: Legendary golems tied to ancient ruins.
  • Latias and Latios: Legendary Dragon/Psychic-type Pokémon from the Mirage Tower.
  • Kyogre, Groudon, and Rayquaza: The legendary titans central to the game's plot.

Important Trainers in Pokémon Emerald

The game features a variety of challenging Trainers, including Gym Leaders, the Elite Four, and other key figures. Here are some of the most important ones:

  • Gym Leaders:
    • Roxanne (Rustboro City, Rock-type)
    • Brawly (Dewford Town, Fighting-type)
    • Wattson (Mauville City, Electric-type)
    • Flannery (Lavaridge Town, Fire-type)
    • Norman (Petalburg City, Normal-type)
    • Winona (Fortree City, Flying-type)
    • Tate and Liza (Mossdeep City, Psychic-type)
    • Wallace (Sootopolis City, Water-type)
  • Elite Four:
    • Sidney (Dark-type)
    • Phoebe (Ghost-type)
    • Glacia (Ice-type)
    • Drake (Dragon-type)
  • Other Key Trainers:
    • Archie (Team Aqua Leader)
    • Maxie (Team Magma Leader)
    • Steven Stone (Champion in Ruby/Sapphire, mentor in Emerald)
    • Battle Frontier Brains (e.g., Scott, Anabel, Tucker, etc., for post-game challenges)
